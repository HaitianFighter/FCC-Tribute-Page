Nikola Tesla

The forgotten father of electricity

These are the defining moments of Mr. Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla was born on July 10th in Smiljan of the Austrian Empire
  • 1870 - Tesla moved to Karlovac and began to attend school at the Higher Real Gymnasium
  • 1882 - Began working for the Continental Edison Company in France
  • 1884 - Emigrated to the United States (NYC) and was hired by Thomas Edison to work at Edison Machine Works
  • 1886 - Left Edison Machine Works and partnered with Robert Lane and Benjamin Vail to create Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing
  • 1887 - Tesla designed an induction motor that ran on alternating current, putting AC on the map
  • 1888 - Beginning of the "Current War" between Tesla's AC system and Edison's DC system
  • 1898 - Demonstrated a radio controlled boat, which he called a teleautomaton. People who bore witness to the event claimed forces such as magic or telpathy controlled the boat.
  • 1906 - Tesla invented Tesla's Oscillator; a steam powered mechanical oscillator
  • 1943 - Nikola Tesla died alone in the New Yorker Hotel

"The evolution of electric power from the discovery of Faraday in 1831 to the initial great installation of the Tesla polyphase system in 1896 [at Niagara Falls] is undoubtedly the most tremendous event in all engineering history."

Dr. Charles F. Scott
